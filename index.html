DREAMWILLOW

Lead Designer and Programmer at WolverineSoft Studio (09/08/2019 - 12/08/2019)

About

DREAMWILLOW is a top-down 2D twin-stick shooter made by the University of Michigan's video game development club, where you resurrect your felled foes to fight alongside you in a dreamlike forest. Click here to view some of the stuff I did on the game, as well as the full post-mortem, including what went right, what went wrong, and what lessons we learned throughout the development process.

Teaching

Being an instructional aide for EECS 280, an intermediate-level programming course. (12/2016 - 12/2017)

As an IA, I was tasked with class organization, writing and grading exams, holding labs and office hours, and answering students' questions online.

In addition to a large team of amazing students, I worked closely with Dr. Drew DeOrio, Dr. Amir Kamil, and Dr. James Juett.

I was also the head of code integrity assurance for the class. This role involved writing scripts and algorithms to compress and compare students' code files across all recent semesters to ensure a fair playing field for students. I would then meet with Dr. DeOrio to analyze the results and write the appropriate reports to the Honor Council.

Game Design

Creating a video game from scratch. (5/2019 - present)

With a team of about 20 dedicated Wolverine Soft artists, programmers, producers, designers and audio engineers, I've had the amazing opportunity to help design and implement a top-down adventure video game to get more Unity experience. Scope management, version control, and communication are core to the project's mission of creating a simple, fun, game with a meaningful message in a comparatively small development cycle. My personal contributions so far range from designing enemy behavior to implementing subtle mechanics for smoother gameplay, like an auto-aim feature for the player.

Projects

FAIC

Using bash and C++, designed a webscraper purposed for gathering and comparing interchange data from various automotive aftermaket companies based on thousands of EO part numbers; results are organized into a parameterized .csv file.

Out of Order Processor

In a five-person team, used SystemVerilog to design a 4-way superscalar R10K out-of-order processor including non-blocking instruction, data, and victim caches, instruction prefetching, sophisticated branch prediction, a return-address stack, a load-store queue and load and store buffers.

In the Nick of Time

Worked in a four-person team to design an FPGA-based educational video game; in assembly language, designed drivers for peripherals including VGA monitor, mouse, keyboard, SD card read/write, camera, microphone and speaker; designed drivers' controls in Verilog.

Priority Encoder

Worked in a three-person group to design a performance-heavy priority encoder at the transistor level using Cadence Virtuoso. Tasks involved logic reduction and synthesis, transistor characteristic optimization, transient analysis and critical path reduction.

Dungeon Floor Generator

Used Unity C# to write a program that randomly generates a dungeon floor layout for general use in dungeon crawler games.

Maze Race

Used Java GUI programming to design a multiplayer computer game where players race to the center of an periodically changing maze.
